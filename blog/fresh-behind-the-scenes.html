What it actually takes to organize a 50-person sport camp on a Mallorca beach

The first thing nobody tells you about organizing a sport camp is that the sport is the easy part.

FRESH 26 had 54 registered participants from 27 countries. Three different arrival days. Four room categories. A hotel that had never hosted a roundnet event before. A WhatsApp group that hit 53 members by day one — plus the organizer group, the coaches group, and the one someone accidentally created at 11pm on night two.

The logistics started four months out. Rooming list: who sleeps with whom, who arrives late, who has a dietary requirement, who paid in full and who was still "checking with their partner." The hotel needed the final list 30 days before arrival. We had people confirming the week before.

Then there's the programme. Beach courts need to be reserved — and in March in Mallorca, you're competing with other sports clubs, local schools, and the occasional German family who didn't read the sign. The tournament bracket depends on skill levels you only find out when people actually arrive. A coaching clinic at 9am sounds reasonable until half your participants were out until 2am celebrating a quarterfinal win.

Payments are their own spreadsheet. People pay from seven different countries, four different currencies, and a surprising number of different transfer methods. One participant paid in three installments from two different bank accounts. We made it work.

What actually makes it work: systems built before things go wrong, a good hotel contact who answers WhatsApp messages on Sunday mornings, and accepting that something will break every day. The question is not whether — it's how fast you can fix it without anyone at the breakfast table noticing.

FRESH 26 sold out eight weeks before the event. Players from Australia, South Korea, Brazil, and most of Europe, in one place for four days. The tournament winner was from Germany. The most improved player was from Japan. The last game on the beach finished at 9pm on the final day, in fading light, with about thirty people watching.

That part never gets old.

FRESH 27 planning starts in autumn. If you want in: futureroundnet.com.
